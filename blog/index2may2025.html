About Billy & Bluma: Double Trouble

In this thrilling sequel, Billy reunites with his childhood friend Bluma, and together they embark on a quest filled with danger, mystery, and divine intervention. When a mischievous monkey snatches Billy’s glowing, indestructible orb—a gift from his immortal parents—and drops it into Bluma’s bread dough, chaos ensues. Their lion guardian, Leonard, bites into the bread, breaks a tooth, and sets off a chain of events that leads the trio on an unforgettable adventure.

Guided by a map and a gold coin bearing the likeness of their beloved Aunt Mathilda, a resurrected tribulation saint, Billy and Bluma sneak out of school to find her. But their journey takes a dark turn when they encounter Junta, a sinister figure who lures kids into his secret cult. With Leonard drugged and shipped off to a hunting ground, the children are locked in an abyss. Through Billy’s divine super strength and Bluma’s courage, they break free, rescue other prisoners, and set out to save Leonard. From borrowing a plane to Billy throwing it into the air, their quest is a rollercoaster of faith and ingenuity, culminating in a heartwarming reunion with Aunt Mathilda.

Discover The Adventures of Billy Lionheart Series

If you’re new to Billy’s world, start with the first book, Billy The Lion Boy, where we meet Billy, a young orphan searching for his immortal parents in the Millennial Kingdom of Christ. Raised by his lion guardian Leonard and lioness Lina in Kangaroo Land, Billy learns to speak animal languages and grows strong wrestling with lion cubs. His journey to Celestial City is filled with heartwarming moments, daring escapes, and a coming-of-age ceremony that unites humans and animals in celebration.

Both books in The Adventures of Billy Lionheart series blend faith-based themes with action-packed storytelling, making them ideal for readers who love epic adventures with a spiritual heart. Explore the series at janicewee.com/billyseries.html and join Billy’s quest!

Emunah Short Stories: Abigail

Review by Miranda P. YEOH

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

December 10, 2024

Have you noticed how strong relationships, partnerships, and marriages often stem from childhood bonds, school friendships, or church connections? This story introduces Abigail, a relatable heroine who isn’t perfectly organized or blessed with striking beauty. Abigail finds herself drawn to a muscular man with worldly values, overlooking the quiet love and patience of Aaron, her steadfast childhood friend. The story turns darker with Stella, Abigail’s boss, and a Sunday Christian. Stella’s animosity toward Abigail and others leads her down a path of hate, culminating in more sinister acts. It raises thought-provoking questions: Are hatred and murder sins of the same family, although they differ in magnitude? How should a believer respond to such trials? This compelling short story offers valuable lessons about gratitude, faith, and the dangers of taking blessings for granted. It’s a poignant reminder to cherish what we already have. Don’t miss it!
